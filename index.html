About CEC English Camp

We provide grammar, reading, speaking, and listening in one integrated experience…

CEC English Camp offers a unique, joy-centered learning journey for children. We believe that language learning should be meaningful, connected to real life, and filled with positive emotional experiences. That’s why our curriculum is designed around short novels, interactive AI tasks, and everyday communication themes.

Our multi-level system (CEFR A1–B2) ensures that each learner starts at their own level and progresses steadily. Weekly programs include thematic vocabulary, listening stories, drama-based role play, grammar coaching, writing prompts, and daily speaking practice with chatbot companions. We support our students through every stage of development with warm, human-centered guidance.

Programs

Interactive English Learning

Engaging activities that teach English through fun games, role plays, and creative interaction. Students learn naturally by doing and speaking.

Reading Comprehension Programs

Short serialized novels help students build vocabulary and understanding, while emotionally engaging plots make reading enjoyable. Includes guided reading, character role-play, and vocabulary extension tasks.

English Grammar

Grammar taught in context. Clear explanations, fun visuals, and immediate practice help students internalize rules with confidence. Includes weekly review quizzes and sentence-building games.

Season 1, Episode 1: The Hidden Warehouse Files

Our journey begins with Episode 1…

A short novel that blends action, humor, and heartwarming moments. In a dusty warehouse, a girl discovers a forgotten robot that changes her life. Each story helps children improve their English while experiencing wonder, empathy, and excitement.

This episode introduces Miso, a curious girl who accidentally activates an old AI robot named Robo. Together, they uncover a past filled with secrets—and begin their English learning adventure. This episode includes an illustrated e-book, listening MP3, role-play practice, and writing journal prompts.
